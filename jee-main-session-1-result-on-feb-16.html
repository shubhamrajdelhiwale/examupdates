JEE Main Session 1 2026 Result: The National Testing Agency (NTA) is scheduled to release the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main session 1 result on February 16, 2026. Students who appeared for the examination will be able to download their results using application number and password on the official website jeemain.nta.nic.in. Along with results, cut-off marks will also be released.

JEE Main 2026 Exam: How To Download Result?

  • Visit the official website jeemain.nta.nic.in.
  • On the homepage, click on "JEE Main Session 1 Result 2026" under "Candidate Activity" board.
  • A new page will open. Enter your application number and password.
  • Click on "Submit" and then click on "Result" section.
  • Your result will be displayed on the screen.
  • Download and save your result for future reference.

Re-Evaluation And Re-Checking Process

Candidates must note that there will be no re-evaluation or re-checking of their scores, and no correspondence regarding the same will be entertained, the NTA has said. Those who were found using Unfair Means Practices (UMP) during the examination will not have their results issued.

JEE Main Session 1 Results 2026 Live Update: How is AIR decided?

Rank, rather than percentile, is the relevant statistic during the admissions process. Once all students have their percentiles calculated by shift, all shifts are compiled together, and one cumulative All India Rank (AIR) is created. This AIR determines eligibility for counselling and whether you receive a college or university education, and which branch of study you will receive or select.

It is possible for a student with less than 100 percent of equal points total for that examination to have a better AIR than another student with a higher score because his or her percentile was also higher.

On the higher end of the scale, percentile differences can translate into huge jumps in rank. Going from the 99th percentile to the 99.5th percentile or from the 99.5th percentile to the 99.9th percentile can change your rank by thousands. This happens when high scorers are bunched together in a small area of scores. Just one more right answer can have a huge effect on how you will be ranked.

JEE Main Exam Calendar 2026 – FAQs

On which dates will JEE Main 2026 be conducted?

Session 1 was held on January 21, 22, 23, 24 and 28, 2026. Session 2 is scheduled from April 2 to April 9, 2026.

Who conducts JEE Main 2026?

The National Testing Agency (NTA) conducts the JEE Main examination in computer-based mode across multiple cities in India and abroad.

How many times is JEE Main conducted in a year?

JEE Main is conducted twice a year – January session and April session. Candidates can appear in one or both sessions.

What is the exam pattern of JEE Main 2026?

The exam consists of three subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Each paper carries 300 marks and includes multiple-choice and numerical value questions.

How is the JEE Main percentile calculated?

The percentile score is calculated based on the relative performance of all candidates in a particular shift. Scores are normalized to ensure fairness across multiple sessions.

Is there negative marking in JEE Main 2026?

Yes, for MCQs, one mark is deducted for each incorrect answer. Numerical value questions may have specific marking rules as per official guidelines.

What is the official website to check JEE Main 2026 result?

The official websites are nta.ac.in and jeemain.nta.nic.in where results, answer keys, and notices are published.

How many candidates appear for JEE Main annually?

Over 9–10 lakh candidates typically register for JEE Main every year across both sessions.

What is the validity of JEE Main 2026 score?

The JEE Main 2026 score is valid for admission in the academic year 2026–27 for NITs, IIITs, GFTIs and other participating institutions.
