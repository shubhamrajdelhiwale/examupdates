KSEAB denies Karnataka 2nd PUC paper leak reports

KSEAB denied 2nd PUC paper leak rumours spreading on social media. Officials warned students not to trust fraudsters asking money for question papers. Exams will be held from February 28 to March 17, 2026.

With only two days remaining for Karnataka’s 2nd PUC exams, rumours about a paper leak are spreading on social media and worrying students.

Karnataka School Examination and Assessment Board (KSEAB) has said clearly that these reports are false. The board confirmed that there is no paper leak and warned students not to believe people who are trying to cheat them by asking for money. Some social media posts and videos claim that they can sell 2nd PUC question papers. A few fake accounts are asking students to pay Rs 100 to get the paper. One message says, "If you pay Rs 100, you will get the question paper." A video called 'Second PUC Exam Question for Wife' is also being shared widely. Other posts mention an account named ‘Delta Kannadiga,’ which is said to be taking money from many accounts in exchange for question papers.

KSEAB President Prakash Nittali has rejected all these claims, as per a News18 Kannada report. He said no one has got access to the question papers illegally. The board is watching such social media activities and will soon complain to the Cyber Police for investigation.

As per the schedule, the 2nd PUC exams will be conducted from February 28 to March 17, 2026, at 1,217 centres. Around 7,10,363 students will appear for the exams. The board has made live webcasting compulsory at all exam centres to ensure smooth and fair conduct.

KSEAB Board Exams 2026 Overview

Karnataka School Examination and Assessment Board (KSEAB) is the primary authority for school-level examinations in Karnataka. It is responsible for setting the KSEAB Board syllabus for Classes 9–12, conducting examinations, and declaring results. The board ensures proper assessment of knowledge and skills among students.

A major update for the KSEAB Board Exam 2026 session is the implementation of three annual examinations — Exam 1, Exam 2, and Exam 3 — for both SSLC and 2nd PUC. This system replaces the earlier supplementary exam format and provides students multiple opportunities to improve their scores without losing an academic year.

KSEAB Board Exam 2026 FAQ

What is the full form of KSEAB? ⌄

KSEAB stands for Karnataka School Examination and Assessment Board, which conducts SSLC (Class 10) and 2nd PUC (Class 12) examinations in Karnataka.

When will the KSEAB board admit card 2026 be released? ⌄

The KSEAB admit card 2026 is expected to be released a few weeks before the examination. Students can collect it from their respective schools once officially issued.

What are the tentative dates for the KSEAB Board Exam 2026? ⌄

The SSLC (Class 10) exams are tentatively scheduled from March 18 to April 1, 2026, while the 2nd PUC (Class 12) exams are expected from February 28 to March 17, 2026.

How many exams will be conducted under the new KSEAB system in 2026? ⌄

From 2026 onwards, KSEAB will conduct three annual examinations — Exam 1, Exam 2, and Exam 3 — allowing students multiple opportunities to improve their scores within the same academic year.
