MPSC Rajyaseva Apply Online 2026

The application process for the MPSC Rajyaseva Recruitment exam will be held through an online platform. The aspirants who had applied for the exam through the MPSC portal. The steps to apply for the MPSC Civil Services exam are explained below:

Step 1: Go to the official website of the Commission, and click on the MPSC online application link that you will find on the header.

Step 2: Once you are redirected to the online application portal, you will need to register yourself. For this, click New User Registration on the top left corner.

Step 3: Fill in your details and verify your registration. Now you will have a Login ID and password to access the site.

Step 4: Login using your credentials, and now you can proceed to fill the MPSC application form by selecting the exam as Rajyaseva Exam.

Step 5: Fill the complete form with all your details, such as personal information, educational qualifications, etc.

Step 6: Once all your details have been filled and checked by you, upload the scanned copies of all the documents that are necessary. These include educational certificates, mark sheets, caste certificate, domicile certificate, etc.

Step 7: Once the complete form is filled, click on submit.

Step 8: You will now be directed to an online payment portal. Pay the MPSC application fee through any of the available modes.

Step 9: Print the MPSC application form, once the payment is successful. Keep it safe, as you will need it in the future.

MPSC Rajyaseva Selection Process 2026

The MPSC Civil Services exam selection process is divided into three stages. The candidates who are selected after the first stage will appear in the second and so on. For an aspirant to get a post at any department of the state of Maharashtra, they will need to cross all the stages of the exam.

Prelims Exam

The prelims exam is a written exam. It is the screening test of the aspirants. The prelims include questions from subjects of general studies and aptitude. One needs to score more than the cut-off marks to get selected for the next stage. The MPSC Rajyaseva Cut-Off marks are decided by the Commission.

  • Prelims exams consist of Paper I and Paper II.
  • The total marks are 400.

Main Exam

The main exam is also a written test. The exam is held so that the knowledge of the selected candidates in the fields such as politics, geography, human resource development, etc. can be judged.

  • The mains exams consist of Paper I, II, III, IV, V, and VI.
  • The total marks are 800.

Interview

The selected candidates from the main exam then appear for a personal interview. The panel judges the candidate on his or her presence of mind, aptitude, mental ability, and field knowledge. The candidates who cross the interview stage will then be recruited to their desired post. The total marks shall be 100.

MPSC Rajyaseva Eligibility 2026

The MPSC Rajyaseva eligibility criteria should be checked by the aspirants before applying for the MPSC Rajyaseva Recruitment exam. If they fail to meet any condition detailed in the criterion, their application will be rejected.

  • The minimum age limit for the posts of Rajyaseva Exams is 19 years.
  • The upper age limit is 38 years for the General candidates.
  • There is some relaxation given in the maximum age limit to some categories.
  • The candidate must possess a bachelor’s degree or its equivalent from any recognized university in India.

MPSC Rajyaseva Admit Card

The Maharashtra State Service Hall Ticket will be released separately for each stage of the selection process. The admit card is made available on the MPSC website a few days before the respective exam. Without the admit card, candidates are not allowed inside the exam hall. It contains details about the exam date, exam centre location, shift timings, and more.

MPSC State Service Answer Key

The Maharashtra State Service Response Sheet is released only for the Prelims Exam. The answer key contains the correct answers to the questions asked in the exam. By referring to the same, they can verify their responses and estimate their scores in the Prelims Exam and get an idea about their chances of qualifying the exam.

MPSC Rajyaseva Cut Off

The MPSC State Service Cut Off is released online on the commission's website in a few weeks after the exam. The cut off is announced category-wise and post-wise. Candidates need to score marks more than the respective post-wise and category-wise cut off to be eligible for further selection.

MPSC Rajyaseva Result

The MPSC State Service Result is announced online on the commission's website only. The result is announced stage-wise. After the prelims result is announced, the shortlisted candidates are called for the Mains. Based on the Mains Result, candidates are called for the interview. After completion of the interview and DV, the final selection list is released on the MPSC website.
