RRB Group D Answer Key 2026: Raise Objection Process

Candidates can raise objections against the provisional RRB Answer Key if they find discrepancies in the officially released responses. To challenge any answer, candidates must log in through the official RRB portal using their credentials and select the question they wish to challenge within the stipulated objection window. Each objection must be supported with valid proof and is usually subject to a fee per question, which is refundable if the objection is accepted. The final Railway Group D Answer Key will be released after reviewing all valid challenges.

How to Raise Objection Against RRB Group D Answer Key 2026?

Candidates who find discrepancies in the provisional RRB Group D Answer Key 2026 can challenge the responses through the official RRB portal. The objection window for the Railway Group D Answer Key is available for a limited period only.

Follow the step-by-step process below to raise objections against the Group D Answer Key 2026:

  • Visit the respective regional RRB official website where you applied.
  • Click on “Viewing of Question Paper, Responses and Keys & Raising of Objections for Level 1 Posts”.
  • Read all the instructions carefully before proceeding.
  • Scroll down to find the login section.
  • Enter your User ID and Password to access the RRB Answer Key portal.
  • Select the question number for which you want to raise an objection.
  • Provide proper explanation and valid supporting documents.
  • Pay the prescribed objection fee per question.
  • Submit the objection form before the deadline.

After reviewing all objections, RRB will release the final RRB Group D Answer Key 2026 on rrb.digialm.com, which will be used for preparing the RRB Group D Result 2026.

RRB Group D Marking Scheme 2026

The RRB Group D 2026 CBT follows a uniform marking pattern across all regions and shifts. Candidates calculating scores using the RRB Group D Answer Key 2026 must follow the official Railway Group D marking scheme given below.

  • The RRB Group D CBT consists of objective-type questions (MCQs).
  • Each correct answer is awarded 1 mark.
  • Negative marking is applicable in the exam.
  • 1/3 mark (0.33) is deducted for every incorrect answer.
  • No marks are deducted for unanswered questions.
  • The marking scheme remains uniform across all RRB zones.
