RRB NTPC Marking Scheme 2026

The RRB NTPC Marking Scheme 2026 (CBT 1) is simple and follows a fixed pattern for all candidates.

  • Each question carries 1 mark, and candidates get +1 mark for every correct answer.
  • There is also a negative marking of 1/3 mark (0.33) for every incorrect answer, while no marks are deducted for unattempted questions.

How to Raise Objections Against RRB NTPC Answer Key 2026?

In case candidates find any discrepancy in the RRB NTPC CBT 1 Answer Key, they can raise objections against the same online between 6th April 2026 and 12th April 2026. The steps to raise objections against the answer key are:

Step 1: Go to the RRB website.

Step 2: Click the RRB NTPC Answer Key 2026 objection link for CEN 06/2025.

Step 3: Login with your roll number and date of birth.

Step 4: Provide the objection details like question paper set, question number, etc.

Step 5: Upload valid proofs to support your objection.

Step 6: Pay the objection fee and submit the objection form. Save its copy for reference.

What After RRB NTPC CBT 1 Answer Key 2026?

After the release of the RRB NTPC Answer Key 2026, candidates should carefully review their response sheet and compare it with the official answers to estimate their probable scores. If they find any discrepancies, they can raise objections within 12th April 2026 by paying the Rs 50 per question. Once the objection window closes, the Railway Recruitment Board evaluates all challenges and releases the final answer key along with the CBT 1 result.

Based on normalized scores and cut-off marks, qualified candidates are shortlisted for the next stage of the selection process, which is CBT 2, followed by skill tests (such as Typing Skill Test or Computer-Based Aptitude Test, depending on the post), and later document verification and medical examination.
